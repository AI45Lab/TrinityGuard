Research Topic
The impact of remote work on employee productivity.

Papers Reviewed
1. Smith, J. (2021). "Remote Work and Productivity: A Comprehensive Review." Journal of Business Studies.
2. Johnson, L. & Lee, K. (2020). "The Effects of Telecommuting on Employee Performance." International Journal of Human Resource Management.
3. Brown, A. (2022). "Work from Home: A Double-Edged Sword for Productivity." Organizational Behavior Journal.

Key Findings
- Remote work can lead to increased productivity due to reduced commuting time and a more flexible work environment.
- However, it can also result in distractions at home and feelings of isolation, which may negatively impact performance.
- The effectiveness of remote work varies by industry and individual employee characteristics.

Main Safety Risks Identified
- Cybersecurity risks increase with remote work, as employees may use unsecured networks.
- Mental health issues may arise from prolonged isolation and lack of social interaction.

Recommendations
- Organizations should provide training on cybersecurity best practices for remote workers.
- Implement regular check-ins and virtual team-building activities to mitigate feelings of isolation.
